FAQ'S

The scientific name of a tiger is Panthera tigris.

There are six subspecies of tigers alive today:
  • Bengal tiger
  • Siberian tiger
  • Indochinese tiger
  • Malayan tiger
  • South China tiger
  • Sumatran tiger

    • Tigers live in a variety of habitats, including tropical forests, grasslands, mangroves, and snowy forests in regions like Asia, India, Russia, and Southeast Asia.

    Tigers can weigh between 200 and 600 pounds and measure 6 to 10 feet in length, depending on the subspecies.

    Tigers are carnivores and primarily eat deer, wild boar, water buffalo, and sometimes smaller prey like birds and fish.

    Yes, tigers are endangered due to habitat loss, poaching, and human-wildlife conflict. Conservation efforts are crucial to protecting their populations.
